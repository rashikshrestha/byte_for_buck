The Storage Buyer's Market: What You're Really Paying For

A data-driven look at 607 Amazon storage listings — comparing price, speed, and value across HDDs, SSDs, portable drives, and tape cartridges.

Every buyer faces the same question: which storage gives me the most for my money? This dashboard cuts through the noise to reveal the real price-performance story.
Market OverviewDrive Type Distribution
Portable HDDs dominate with 40% of listings, but SSDs (32%) are closing the gap fast.
Cost AnalysisMedian Price per TB by Drive Type
Tape is the cheapest per-TB option at ~$9.66/TB, but SSDs and HDDs trade blows near $22–$26/TB.
Performance vs CostRead Speed (MB/s) vs Price per TB — colored by drive type
SSDs cluster in the 500–7000 MB/s band at comparable prices to HDDs. HDDs show wide speed variance due to mixed RPM ratings in the dataset. Portable drives sit low-and-left: cheap but slow.
Speed BenchmarkAverage Read Speed by Drive Type (MB/s)
SSDs average 724 MB/s read — nearly 4× a portable HDD's 197 MB/s. For workloads that need throughput, the SSD premium is justified.
Condition MarketNew vs Used — Count & Avg Price per TB
The market is nearly split 50/50 between new and used drives. Buying used can cut your cost-per-TB significantly — worth considering for bulk storage.
Brand LandscapeTop 8 Brands by Listing Count
Seagate leads among named brands with 54 listings. A large share of listings (409) have no identified brand — a quality-signal gap for buyers.
Capacity DistributionHow Many Drives Are Available at Each Size?
The sweet spot for supply is 1–4 TB — the most competition means the best pricing for everyday buyers.
Buyer SatisfactionAverage Rating by Drive Type
All categories hover between 4.0–4.6 ★, suggesting buyers are generally satisfied regardless of type. Ratings alone won't differentiate your choice.
